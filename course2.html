FPGA for Beginners

Course Information

Instructor: Engineer Amin Khadem Al-Husseini
Duration: 5 hours
Level: Intermediate
Price: Free

About This Course

FPGA (Field-Programmable Gate Array) is a powerful technology that allows you to design and implement custom digital hardware. This course provides a comprehensive introduction to FPGA design, from basic concepts to practical implementation.

You'll learn how to use hardware description languages (HDL), design digital circuits, and implement them on FPGA boards. The course covers essential topics like combinational logic, sequential circuits, and advanced FPGA features.

Course Content & Chapters

Part 1: Introduction to FPGA

▼
Learn the fundamentals of FPGA technology, including architecture, applications, and development tools. Understand how FPGAs differ from traditional microcontrollers and when to use them.

Part 2: Hardware Description Languages

▼
Introduction to VHDL and Verilog. Learn the syntax, structure, and best practices for describing digital hardware.

Part 3: Digital Design Fundamentals

▼
Master combinational and sequential logic design. Learn about gates, flip-flops, and state machines.

Part 4: FPGA Implementation

▼
Learn how to synthesize, place, and route your designs on FPGA hardware. Understand timing constraints and optimization.

Part 5: Advanced Projects

▼
Apply your knowledge to real-world projects. Design and implement complex digital systems on FPGA.
